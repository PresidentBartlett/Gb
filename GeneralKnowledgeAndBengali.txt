To gently correct the record before we dive into the engineering: Bangladesh actually has **8 administrative divisions** and **64 districts** (not 6). Furthermore, if a user asks who the Prime Minister is right now in June 2026, the model must know that **Tarique Rahman** was sworn in on February 17, 2026, following the interim government led by Muhammad Yunus.
If the model gets those wrong, users will immediately assume your e-Passport and NID data are also hallucinations.
Here is the developer's blueprint for injecting "soft" or "general" cultural knowledge into an AI's brain so it feels authentically grounded in Bangladesh. You achieve this using a three-layered architecture:
### Layer 1: The "Worldview" System Anchor (Static Facts)
You cannot rely on the model's pre-training data because pre-training data is inherently Western-centric and outdated. You must hard-code the immutable geographical and cultural baselines directly into the AI's overarching "System Instructions" (or the system prompt of your RAG router).
You inject a tight, token-efficient **"Worldview Block"** that the model reads before answering *any* question.
**Example Injection:**
> **Geopolitical & Cultural Anchor (Bangladesh):**
>  * **Geography:** 8 Divisions (Dhaka, Chattogram, Rajshahi, Khulna, Barishal, Sylhet, Rangpur, Mymensingh), 64 Districts, and 495 Upazilas.
>  * **Capital:** Dhaka.
>  * **National Symbols:** The flag is a red disc on a green field. The national anthem is "Amar Sonar Bangla."
>  * **Currency:** Bangladeshi Taka (BDT). Never use Rupees (INR) or Dollars as a default context.
>  * **Tone/Culture:** Understand that administrative procedures often involve localized intermediaries ("Dalals"). Address users with respect, utilizing culturally appropriate terms when relevant.
> 
*Why this works:* It forces the model to treat these facts as absolute laws of physics. It will never invent a 9th division because the System Prompt explicitly caps it at 8.
### Layer 2: Live Search Grounding (Dynamic Facts)
You should **never** hardcode dynamic political facts (like the name of the Prime Minister, current exchange rates, or the latest budget tax slabs) into your database. Politics and economies change too fast.
Instead, you use **Tool Calling / Grounding**. In Google AI Studio, this is the "Grounding with Google Search" toggle you turned on earlier. In your production app (ProofSheet), you will configure your AI agent to recognize "time-sensitive" queries.
 * **How it works:** If a user asks, *"Who is the PM?"*, the agent recognizes this isn't in your JSON database. It bypasses your OpenSearch vector database, triggers a live Google Search, reads the news from 2026, and reports back correctly.

 3. **Speaker Diarization (Optional but powerful):** If your app involves users uploading recordings of bureaucratic disputes, use **PyAnnote** layered over Whisper to identify *who* is speaking (e.g., separating the Citizen's voice from the Desk Officer's voice).
